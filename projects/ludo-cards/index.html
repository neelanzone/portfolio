Ludo Cards

UX-UI Game Design

Classic Ludo often leaves too much to chance, which elevates tension before the dice roll but limits tactical depth and player agency.

RPG mechanics, narrative framing, and deck-based abilities were layered onto the familiar loop so each turn could reward foresight instead of a single lucky throw.

Intentional, immersive play rooted in decision making, tradeoff acknowledgement, and player agency.

Narrative
Design

The building blocks of a basic story - the hero's journey, factions, spatial context - already exist in Classic Ludo. We just never saw it that way. We rolled dice, raced pawns, and took out competition when the dice gods let us. It is nostalgic, and I felt something was missing - lore.

Mountains Oceans Valleys Sky

Worldbuilding Hooks

Terrain names turned each lane into a setting cue, which helped movement feel more like traversal through regions than a color-coded sprint.

Narrative Payoff

That framing opened room for quests, regional hazards, and card abilities that could feel anchored to the world instead of randomly assigned to a pawn.

Board Game
Design

Ludo Cards was born from the idea of adding RPG elements to Classic Ludo. A familiar game gets more interesting when players can plan instead of only react.
