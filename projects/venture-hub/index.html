Venture Hub

UXProduct DesignConcept

Across four campuses, 3200 students were running 500 to 600 venture projects in a single academic year, supported by 100 faculty and governed by a 30-member management team. Progress lived in email chains. Without a shared system, blockers stayed hidden, promising projects missed timely support, and management had little reliable visibility into what was actually moving.

Research showed mentorship lived in a grey zone for faculty. It was expected, but barely incentivised. So we reduced dependence on frequent intervention, giving students a venture feed, job board, async faculty support, and real-time AI feedback. While management received smart alerts to spot blockers early and identify ventures worth backing.

A system that rewards autonomy. Progress made visible without regular manual reporting. A built-in portfolio builder gives students a meaningful reason to stay engaged, while management gets a bird’s-eye view of venture activity, momentum, and health.

The system succeeds only when everyone has a reason to keep using it. For students, it must feel engaging, not watchful. For management, it must offer enough clarity and value to keep the platform active, relevant, and worth investing in.

Process

Map Stakeholder Views

Different users needed different levels of detail, so the system began with role-specific visibility needs.

Ventures Mentors Admins Reviewers

Role Visibility

Each stakeholder needed a different zoom level, from portfolio-wide health to venture-by-venture status, guidance, and next actions.

Shared Frame

Mapping those views first made it easier to design one system that could support coaching, oversight, and reporting without splitting the source of truth.

Organize Venture Signals

Progress indicators, milestones, and updates were structured so teams and reviewers could read momentum quickly.

MilestonesRisksMomentumDependencies

Signal Hierarchy

The interface groups venture updates into signals that can be scanned quickly, so health, blockers, and movement become legible without opening multiple reports.

Readable Progress

Once signals are consistent, mentors and administrators can interpret the same venture state from the same view instead of translating between different tools or formats.

Design Progress Workflows

The experience was shaped around actionable views that support review, guidance, and ongoing program management.

Workflow loop

Phase 01 Weekly updates Live
Phase 02 Mentor review In Progress
Phase 03 Program action TBD

Actionable Reviews

The platform was framed around what should happen next, not only what has already been logged, so reviews can move directly into guidance and follow-up.

Operating Layer

That workflow shift turns the dashboard into a live coordination layer for the program instead of a passive status board used only during checkpoints.

Outcome

Shared Visibility

Stakeholders get a clearer common view of venture progress, making it easier to align guidance, expectations, and timing.

Easier Coordination

Updates, reviews, and interventions can happen from the same system layer instead of being stitched together across scattered tools.

Clearer Momentum

Milestones and blockers become easier to interpret over time, helping teams and mentors see where progress is genuinely moving.

Stronger Oversight

Program-level monitoring becomes more structured and dependable, without losing the detail needed for venture-specific support.
