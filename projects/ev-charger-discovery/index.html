EV Charger Discovery

Scope

Discovery + Interface

Users

Urban EV drivers

This concept reframes charger discovery for gig workers and urban EV users as a trust-and-timing problem. Instead of only helping users find a station, it helps them judge whether that station is worth committing to in the first place.

Overview

Quick read

Problem

Nearby chargers can still be the wrong choice when queues, reliability, and wait conditions are unclear.

Approach

Explored a map-first interface that foregrounds queue status, station credibility, and decision confidence.

Outcome

The result is a clearer discovery layer built around trust, scan speed, and lower decision friction.

Field note

Had to spend an hour at a charging station because of the long queues.

Service friction

The first charger was broken, so was the second. I had to drive around to find one that was working.

Breakdown moment

The battery swapping station was under maintenance, the next station was 2kms away.

Context

Charging decisions break down when uncertainty stacks up

The challenge is not only locating a charger, but deciding whether it is reliable enough to justify the detour. That uncertainty hits hardest for time-sensitive users whose routines depend on predictable charging.
