Poster x Editorial Wave

Designing confidence into every charging stop.

A hybrid of museum-scale typography and cleaner editorial structure, with a desktop-only ambient pixel field that loosens outward from the center and responds to scroll like a soft wave.

Overview

The fast read, with more air.

Problem

Users could find chargers, but not judge route-worthiness, wait risk, or station trust quickly enough.

Approach

The interface reframed discovery around confidence signals, availability context, and scan speed.

Outcome

The result feels sharper and more premium than a plain case study without becoming visually heavy.

Context

Charging choices fail before arrival.

For urban EV users, the nearest charger is often not the best charger. What matters is whether a station is available enough, credible enough, and efficient enough to justify the stop.

This direction gives that story poster-scale framing up front, then settles into a calmer, more readable case-study body.

Artifacts

Map confidence layer

Availability and queue cues surface earlier, before users commit to the route change.

Decision detail

Station pages explain why a charger is worth choosing right now, not just where it sits.

Outcome

A more cinematic shell, without losing readability.

This hybrid is strongest when you want the project pages to feel elevated and memorable while keeping the content structure recruiter-friendly.
