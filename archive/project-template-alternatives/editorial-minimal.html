Editorial Minimal / EV Charger Discovery

Designing confidence into every charging stop.

A calm, recruiter-friendly case-study format with warm surfaces, measured typography, and image-first storytelling.

Overview

The quick read.

Problem

Drivers lacked trustworthy signals for queue length, charger status, and route worthiness.

Approach

The interface elevated confidence cues before users committed to a station.

Outcome

The result reduced decision friction and reframed discovery as a trust problem.

Context

Charging choices break long before arrival.

In dense urban trips, the closest charger is often not the best charger. Users need to judge reliability, wait conditions, and route cost before they divert.

Artifacts

Map confidence layer

Availability and queue cues surfaced earlier in the decision flow.

Station comparison

Fast scan hierarchy made route tradeoffs feel legible under pressure.

Decision detail

Station pages clarified why a stop was worth taking right now.

Outcome

A quieter, sharper case-study shell.

This direction prioritizes readability over spectacle. It is meant for long-form portfolio storytelling with minimal visual noise.
