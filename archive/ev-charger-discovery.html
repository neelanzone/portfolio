EV Charger Discovery

Designing a charger discovery experience for gig workers and urban EV users who needed clearer availability, faster comparison, and more confidence before committing to a stop.

Role Product Designer
Scope Discovery + Interface
Users Urban EV Drivers
Focus Queue Transparency
Overview Context Process Artifacts Outcome More Projects

Overview

The fast read.

This concept reframed charger discovery as a trust-and-timing problem. Instead of only helping users locate a station, it helps them decide whether that station is worth committing to in the first place.

Problem

Nearby chargers can still be the wrong choice when queues, reliability, and wait conditions are unclear.

Approach

I explored a map-first interface that foregrounds queue status, station credibility, and decision confidence.

Outcome

The result is a clearer discovery layer built around trust, scan speed, and lower decision friction.

Context

Charging decisions break down when uncertainty stacks up.

For urban EV users, the issue is not just finding a charger. It is judging whether a station is available enough, credible enough, and efficient enough to justify the detour.

That uncertainty matters even more for gig workers, whose route changes, idle time, and earnings are all directly shaped by charging downtime.

Process

01

Prioritize trust signals

Queue visibility, charger status, and reliability cues were treated as core UI, not secondary metadata.

02

Reduce scan time

The hierarchy was designed so users could compare stations quickly without drilling into every option.

03

Support faster commitment

The interaction model was built to make route tradeoffs and queue risk legible before arrival.
